Part 1: Infinitiv mit zu (Infinitive with "zu")

üìö What is an Infinitive?

The infinitive is the base form of a verb - the dictionary form that always ends in -en (or just -n).

Examples:

  • helfen - to help
  • lesen - to read
  • spielen - to play
  • arbeiten - to work
  • machen - to do/make

üí° When do we use Infinitive with "zu"?

We use infinitive with "zu" when one action in the sentence refers to another action.

Think of it like English "to" - "I begin to run", "I plan to study"

Example Construction:

Ich laufe jeden Tag.

I run every day.

+

Ich beginne.

I begin.

=

Ich beginne, jeden Tag zu laufen.

I begin to run every day.

üéØ Basic Concept

In German, many sentences have two verbs:

  • First verb: Conjugated (changes with subject)
  • Second verb: Infinitive with "zu"
