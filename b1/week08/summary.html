Week 08 Summary: Wenn & Weil

🎯 Quick Reference: Conditional and Causal Clauses

🔑 Key Conjunctions

Word Meaning Type Word Order
wenn when, if Subordinate Verb → END
weil because Subordinate Verb → END
denn because Coordinate Normal (V2)

⚠️ Don't Confuse!

Wenn → when, if (conjunction)
Wann → when? (question)
Wen → whom? (accusative)

📐 Word Order Patterns

Wenn / Weil (Subordinate)

Pattern 1:
Main clause, wenn/weil + ... + VERB

Pattern 2:
Wenn/Weil + ... + VERB, VERB + ...

Denn (Coordinate)

Main clause, denn + normal order

✅ Quick Examples

Wenn (when/if)

Wenn du kommst, koche ich.

When/If you come, I'll cook.

Weil (because)

Ich bleibe zu Hause, weil es regnet.

I stay home because it's raining.

Denn (because)

Ich bleibe zu Hause, denn es regnet.

I stay home because it's raining.

🔄 Weil vs Denn

Same meaning, different word order!

✅ ...weil ich krank bin. (verb END)

✅ ...denn ich bin krank. (normal)

🎯 Special Cases

Modal Verbs

...wenn ich arbeiten muss

Modal verb at end, infinitive before it

Separable Verbs

...weil ich früh aufstehe

Verb stays together at end

Perfect Tense

...wenn ich gegessen habe

haben/sein at end, Partizip before it

🧠 Memory Tricks

  • Wenn: Condition or repeated time
  • Weil: Answers "Warum?" (Why?) → Reason
  • W-END: Wenn/Weil = verb at END
  • Denn: Rhymes with "then" → normal order

📝 Quick Formula

Subordinate Clauses (wenn/weil)

Connector + Subject + Other Elements + VERB

wenn ich Zeit habe

weil es kalt ist

When Sub-clause Comes First

Sub-clause, VERB + Subject + ...

Wenn ich Zeit habe, gehe ich spazieren.

⚡ Common Patterns

  • Wenn ich Zeit habe, ...
  • Wenn es regnet, ...
  • ...weil ich müde bin
  • ...weil ich arbeiten muss
  • ...denn ich bin krank
